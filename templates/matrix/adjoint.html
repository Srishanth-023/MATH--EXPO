The adjoint (or adjugate) of a square matrix is the transpose of the cofactor matrix. For a matrix $A$, the adjoint is denoted by $adj(A)$. If $A$ is invertible, then $A^{-1} = \frac{1}{det(A)} \cdot adj(A)$.
